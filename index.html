-There are various ways you can apply to college, but for our sake most colleges have agreed to accept a couple of "universal" applications. The most well known ones are the Common Application, and the Coalition Application. Now even though there are a lot of colleges that use these applications, not all of them do. We suggest you double-check which applications your school accepts, and if they don't accept any, they usually have their own custom application for you to fill out. Check out the Common App and the Coalition app! The Common App The Coalition App

-There are various types of college degrees out there. The most well-known ones are the Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Arts. These are awarded in most four year colleges. There are also Associate Degrees, which are awarded for a two year study period in most community colleges and even in other public and private colleges. There are also the graduate level degrees, such as a Masters degree and a Doctorate, so you shouldn't worry too much about them as an undergrad. Although these are graduate level degrees, there are some schools who offer programs to shorten the amount of time to achieve such degree. You should check with colleges to make sure they offer these types of programs. Learn more about college degrees

-Usually when it comes to getting your degree you are thinking about your major. When you pick a major in college you usually are getting your degree through that major. Minors are a little different as in usually you will not be getting a degree through that. Instead minors are chosen to either enhance your degree or to study something else you are interested about. Check with colleges to see how they handle minors, as they vary from college to college. Learn more about majors and minors

When it comes it comes to applying to college one can apply with a regular application (at the regular time) or you can apply earlier. Applying to college earlier than usual can come with various benefits, such as knowing about your admission status earlier, having fewer competitors when applying and having the rest of the year stress-free! Now with advantages come disadvantages, such as having to work twice as much to get applications earlier. While applying earlier can be great, there are two ways to apply early. Early decision and early action are different applications and it is very important to know the difference between the two. Early decision are for people who are one hundred percent decided on which college they want to go. This application can only be used in one college as it is binding: if the college accepts the application one must attend that college and cannot refuse. Early action on the other hand allows you to also apply early while not being a binding application. Benefits between ED and EA here

-College requirements vary a lot between the college you are applying for, but most of them share some basic requirements. Here are some of them: a standarized test like the SAT or the ACT,a high school transcript, certain SAT subject tests depending on which concentration you are choosing in college (or an equivalent), a FAFSA application that colleges use in addition to help them decide your financial aid, and an application for which to apply for the college. Check out the SAT here Check out the FAFSA application here Common app for colleges

-Financial aid is a very important topic when it comes to college. Almost all colleges require the student to fill out a FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) if the student wishes for financial aid. Finacial aid can come in various forms. Some of them come as grants, loans, or work-study. Grants do not have to be repaid and are basically free money, while loans must be payed back eventually. Work-study is a different type of aid that basically gives the student some form of job which in turns helps pay for college. Check out the FAFSA application here Learn more about federal aid

-Scholarships are another type of financial aid, maybe the most popular too. They provide free money to attend college for a variaty of reasons. There is a scholarship for almost anything! The only problem is that they can be hard to find, but thankfully now it shouldn't be. Find scholarships here! Find scholarships here too!

-Finding the college for you is one of the hardest tasks. There are some things you should consider though, such as the majors the college offers, how good the programs are in that college, whether you want to stay close home or are willing to go far away, how much aid the college offers, whether you are comfortable with the environment of the college, and many more! These are just some questions and things to consider when choosing a college. Some things to consider for college

-Interviews can be nerve wrecking, but they really shoudln't be. One should treat them as normal conversations, because they are! They aren't your typical conversations though, as you will not be talking like you talk to your family or friends. One must act very respectful and talk properly, bring all necessary documents, and know what you are talking about. Interviews aren't necessary to get into college, but they can be very helpful as it lets the college know who you really are. Check with your colleges if they offer interviews. Tips for interviews

-Deadlines are hard to keep up with. That is why we have compiled some of the most common deadlines in our calendar. To get to our calendar, click on the word "Deadlines" at the top of the page. If you want to keep up with your own deadlines, we suggest you make your own calendar and write such deadlines down. Forgetting you have a calendar is really easy, but if you check such calendar every day can greatly help you keep track of yourself. If such calendar is digital, make sure to set some form of alarm a week or so before said deadline. Good luck!
